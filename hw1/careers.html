What Does a Web Developer Do?

Web developers create and maintain websites that people use every day. They combine technical skills with creativity to build webpages that are functional, easy to use, and visually appealing. Depending on their role, web developers may focus on designing the appearance of a website, building its featrures, or managing how it works behind the scenes.

Web development offers many career opportunities because businesses, schools, healthcare organizations, and other industries rely on websites to share information and provide online services. As technology continues to grow, the demand for skilled web developers continues to increase.

Types of Web Developers

Front-end Developer

Builds the parts of a website that users see and interact with.

Back-end Developer

Develops the server, database, and behind-the-scenes features.

Full-stack Developer

Works with both the front-end and back-end of a website.
